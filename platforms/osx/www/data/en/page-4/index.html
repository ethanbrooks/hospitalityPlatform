Art With Optimism

Full of colors and optimism inaugurated the latest art show of Cartooch, Casco Viejo (Panama) artists and residents, at the ruins of the former Union Club. Alegories of all sorts, some quite sexy! (photos not included&#8230;!). Probably the most refreshing thing about their art is that when it chooses to be critical it does so without cinicism. A daring position for an artist, an edge where people could or not take them seriously.  Pieces related to being different within the society and how people are perceived from different points of view, resources abuse (energy), and freedom in general cruise through their work in an almost cheerful way.  Reminding us of our flaws, but also that it can all be overcome.

Barza vs Real Madrid = Casco Viejo Madness

Panama Casco Viejo, Casco Antiguo I really can´t tell why. Why do we care so very much about either Barza or the Real Madrid. I mean, after all, we are in Panama. Is it because we were a Spanish colony? don´t think so.  But we love them, and depending on which street you are in Casco Viejo you are either a Barza fan or a Real Madrid addict. We were peacefully sitting at our Arco office, which happens to be steps away from a sports bar. At some point we heard this incredible screaming, like a pack of howler monkeys and then a lot of laughter.  I went out to see what was going on, people spilling from inside the place to the street, each one with a theory of what was happening.  The center of the dispute was that the game was still tied after quite a while, nobody could believe it.  And when it ended up tied, people where screaming even higher!!! I don´t know if the Barza or the Real Madrid are aware of the crazy fans they have here.  Someone please send them this blog!

Be my Valentine!

Panama, Casco Viejo, Casco Antiguo This year, St. Valentines is basically at the same time as carnivals. If you are a Panamanian, you know what this means: the city is going to be empty. Awesome. And what a better way to celebrate an intimate weekend with your beloved one than staying in Casco. Think about it: no one will be around, you can sleep in late, walk (not take a car, ever), sunbathe at the beach or picnic there, and have a beautiful dinner at a place like Las Clementinas (228.7617 / events@lasclementinas.com) , which has the most romantic garden in town. A full weekend strolling hand by hand? sounds like a wonderful idea!
